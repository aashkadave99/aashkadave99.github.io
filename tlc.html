The analysis of the two map visualizations reveals interesting insights about the distribution of fares in different areas: Drop-Off Locations Map: The highest fares are concentrated in areas such as Manhattan, Staten Island, and EWR (Newark Liberty International Airport). This concentration of high fares in Manhattan can be attributed to the borough's bustling business districts, upscale neighborhoods, and tourist attractions. Staten Island, known for its suburban nature and ferry terminal, also sees higher fares, likely due to longer distances traveled. EWR being an international airport naturally accounts for high fares, often associated with airport travel. Pickup Locations Map: In contrast, the highest fares for pickup locations are primarily around Queens (likely John F. Kennedy International Airport) and EWR (Newark Liberty International Airport). These locations are major airports in the New York City area, and high fares here are common due to longer trips to and from the airports, airport surcharges, and luggage fees. The concentration of high fares around airports indicates that airport trips significantly contribute to overall fare amounts in the dataset. In summary, the maps illustrate that airport trips, particularly to and from JFK and EWR, tend to have the highest fares, while Manhattan also stands out as a prominent area for high fares during drop-offs. This information can be valuable for taxi service providers and travelers looking to understand fare distribution in different parts of the city.
